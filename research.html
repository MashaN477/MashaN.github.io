Research & Academic Work

Bridging microbiology, immunology, and holistic health perspectives

Academic Background

I'm currently pursuing studies in microbiology and infectious disease, with a focus on the holistic factors that influence immune function and disease outcomes. My academic work explores the traditional foundations of microbiology while my independent research investigates the broader context of health.

Primary Research Interests

Holistic Epidemiology of Immunity

Exploring how the immune system is influenced by factors often overlooked in traditional epidemiology:

  • The impact of chronic stress and cortisol dysregulation on immune function
  • Social determinants of immunity: how community connection affects disease resistance
  • Nutritional status and micronutrient deficiencies in immune response
  • Chemical exposures and toxic burden as immune modulators
  • The role of overall disease state and inflammatory load in susceptibility to infection

The Microbiome as Foundation of Health

My passion lies in understanding gut health as the cornerstone of human wellness:

  • Gut-brain axis and its implications for mental health
  • Microbiome influence on immune development and regulation
  • The role of dysbiosis in chronic disease
  • Dietary interventions for microbiome restoration
  • Long-term effects of antibiotic use on microbial ecosystems

Fascia as a Communication Network

Investigating the fascia network as more than structural tissue:

  • Fascia's role in whole-body communication and integration
  • The biological basis of embodied consciousness
  • Fascial networks and immune function
  • Somatic approaches to trauma and healing
  • Connections between the fascial system and the microbiome
